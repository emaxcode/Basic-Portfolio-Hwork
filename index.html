About Me


Hi! Thank you for visiting my page.

I was born and raised in the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico. As cliche as it sounds, I still love the beach and anything coconut. When I was 14, my family and I moved to Phoenix, Arizona. Funny how quickly you can learn a new language when you really have to.

Fast forward thru the awkward high school years, and I ended up at Arizona State University. As a freshman, I got a job as a projects clerk for a major law firm. I was just a low-pay employee sifting thru files in a dark basement. The job was insanely monotonous, until I started working on the firm's first database to track the huge amount of documents gathered for trial. It was a very basic Access database, but the boss was happy and realized how much I enjoyed technology. I had plans to go to law school, so I graduated with a Justice Studies degree and life went on.

After college, I worked as a county and then a federal probation officer. Looking back, I spent a lot of time fixing co-workers' computer problems or creating spreadsheets and charts for management. My passion for the criminal system faded after a few years, and I took a leap and quit to start a business with my husband. During that time I also had various other jobs doing sales for tech companies, had my daughter, and moved thru the country a few times before settling in Austin.

At some point I realized I wanted to establish a career, but I also wanted a challenge, and to learn something new. So here I am. Coding bootcamp. Looking forward to the next chapter.
